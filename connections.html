Scarborough Fair

Larches are a type of conifer tree. They look similar to other conifers (e.g. pine trees...) but they are deciduous.

Some believe that "Parsely, Sage, Rosemary, and Thyme" is actually a mondegreen for "ne'er a rose grows merry in time." Perhaps it's no coincidence that time-related concepts feature in this puzzle.

The original version of this puzzle had the word "knight" but then I realized that it fit into two of the groups.

Homonyms

While I was beta-testing this puzzle, the real Connections puzzle had a similar category, and it used the word 'gobble'.

One of the trickiest Connections categories is "possible meanings of [word]." My all-time favorite Connections group was in this category ("BAD REVIEW, CAMERA MOVEMENT, COOKWARE, SATYR": Possible meanings of "PAN.") The green group for this puzzle is also a "possible meanings of [word]".

Key: e.g., C Major.

Alternate ideas for the purple group were 'doctor's office' and 'psoriasis'.

I was going to use "armor" but I realized there is a type of maille that works for another group. Hence, "suit of armor" (which is almost always consistent with the blue group).

The first thing to go for at the buffet...

Alternates for this category were "long bones" and "skull."
